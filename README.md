# Global Economic and Demographic Indicators Analysis

**64 years of observations, 20 datasets and 1 result.**  
This project explores global economic and demographic indicators using datasets from the World Bank. It includes data cleaning, exploratory analysis, SQL practice, and an interactive Power BI dashboard.

**Dashboard link** - https://app.powerbi.com/reportEmbed?reportId=ca5e55ed-486e-49a0-9e52-40731bab299a&autoAuth=true&ctid=7631cd62-5187-4e15-8b8e-ef653e366e7a

## Some Interesting Insights:
- In 2024, Africa's population will account for 19% of the total, while its GDP will account for only 2.5% of global GDP.
- The biggest gap: in 2023, the life expectancy of men in Ukraine (66.9 years) is expected to be 13 years less than that of women (80.2 years).
- The poverty rate fell from 47.1% in 1981 to 10.3% in 2024.
- In 2004, nine European countries were among the top ten countries in terms of GDP per capita. In 2024, there will be only five.
- In the year Poland joined the EU (2004), the unemployment rate reached 19%. In 2024, this figure stood at 2.8%.
